Budgeting Basics

Budgeting helps you control fluctuating income and manage expenses, especially as a student. (Bouchrika, 2024)

Use a tool or app to track spending and build sustainable habits. ("How to Budget in College", 2020)

Emergency Funds

Emergency funds prevent students from falling into debt. Start small and save regularly. (Watts, 2024)

Build up to $500, then $1,000 as your goal.

Smart Housing Decisions

Compare on-campus and off-campus living with hidden costs in mind. (Wood, 2023)

Use our housing worksheet to evaluate what's best for you.

Understanding Student Loans

Know the difference between federal and private loans based on your goals. (Martin, 2024)

Be aware of what you borrow—many students misunderstand repayment terms. (Interview, Cobb, 2025)

Building Good Credit

Good credit opens doors later in life. Start early and manage responsibly. (Trip Astute, 2019)

Real example: Misuse can lead to long-term damage. (Interview, Mac, 2025)

Long-Term Financial Habits

Early financial literacy reduces risky behavior and increases confidence. (Xiao et al., 2014; Gomez et al., 2023)

Use a 5-year plan to understand real expenses. (Interview, Mac, 2025)

Tools and Resources

Take advantage of financial advisors and budgeting tools offered on campus. (Interview, Mac, 2025)
